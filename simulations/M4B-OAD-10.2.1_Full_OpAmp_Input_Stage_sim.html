NMOS vs PMOS: Noise Spectrum Comparison

NMOS input pair
PMOS input pair (same ISS)
NMOS has higher gm (by µn/µp ratio) → lower thermal noise. But NMOS has much higher KF → higher 1/f corner frequency. PMOS has lower gm but far lower KF → lower 1/f noise floor, much lower fc. For audio and DC precision: PMOS wins below ~10kHz. For RF and wideband: NMOS wins at high frequencies.

The N-pair is active when Vicm ≥ Vicm_min = Vth_n + 2·Vov. The P-pair is active when Vicm ≤ Vicm_max = VDD − |Vth_p| − 2·Vov. In the overlap region both pairs contribute, making total Gm larger. The dashed green line shows the target constant Gm (e.g., gm1N for single-pair operation). The tail-tripling technique reduces current in each pair during overlap to maintain constant Gm.
