Voltage Budget Bar Chart

Output swing is limited below by Vov_M5 + Vov_M2 (NMOS stack) and above by |Vov_M4| (PMOS load). The cascode load option adds one more Vov of headroom loss at the top. ICMR is bounded below by Vth_n + Vov_M1 + Vov_M5 and above by VDD − |Vth_p| − |Vov_M3| + Vth_n. Reduce Vov to improve ICMR and swing at the cost of lower gm/ISS ratio and more susceptibility to mismatch.

Differential Output Current ΔIout vs Vid

ΔIout (exact square-law)
Linear (gm1·Vid)
Input amplitude A
The square-law result: ΔIout = ISS × (Vid/√2·Vov) × √(1 − (Vid/√2·Vov)²). It is linear near Vid = 0 (slope = gm1 = ISS/Vov), then compresses and saturates at ±ISS when |Vid| = √2·Vov. The 1 dB compression point occurs at |Vid| ≈ 0.4·Vov. THD ≈ (A/4Vov)² for a single-tone sinusoidal input.

Swing & ICMR vs VDD

50% VDD reference
At low VDD, both swing and ICMR shrink. The swing scales as VDD − 3·Vov, so it hits zero when VDD = 3·Vov (minimum viable VDD). The ICMR lower limit is fixed by Vth + 2·Vov and does NOT scale with VDD — it is an absolute floor. Below ~1.0–1.2V on many processes, the NMOS-input 5T OTA cannot achieve an ICMR that includes VDD/2.
