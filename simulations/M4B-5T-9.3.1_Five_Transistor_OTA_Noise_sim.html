Total noise: Svn(f) = (8kTγ/gm1)·(1 + gm3/gm1) · (1 + fc/f). Below fc the noise density rises as 1/f (3 dB/octave). Above fc it is flat (thermal noise floor). Increase W×L to move fc to lower frequencies. Use PMOS input (lower KF) for audio/DC precision.

Each curve: Vn = √(8kTγ/gm1 × 2) = √(16kTγ × Vov / ISS). Power = VDD × ISS. The noise scales as 1/√Power at fixed Vov. Reducing Vov shifts the entire curve down — lower noise at the same power. The noise-power product NPP = 16kTγ × Vov × VDD is independent of ISS; it improves only by reducing Vov or VDD.

Noise Spectral Density + Integration Band

Total noise density (nV/√Hz)
Integration band shading
fc marker
The shaded region shows the integration bandwidth [fLow, fHigh]. The integrated noise² = Svn_th × [ (fHigh − fLow) + fc × ln(fHigh/fLow) ]. When fc ≫ fLow, the 1/f term dominates and the integrated noise grows with fc × ln(fHigh/fLow). Reduce fc by increasing W×L (fc ∝ 1/(W×L)) or switching to PMOS (lower KF).
