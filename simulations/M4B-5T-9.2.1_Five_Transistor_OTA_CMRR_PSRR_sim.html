Each CMRR curve rolls off at −20 dB/decade above the pole frequency fp = 1/(2π·RSS·Cs). The cascode tail has an RSS many orders of magnitude larger, pushing its pole to very low frequencies (often sub-kHz). Above the pole, CMRR = gm1/(π·f·Cs) — independent of RSS. The mismatch floor (dashed) sets a hard limit regardless of tail quality: CMRR_mm = Vov/ΔVth.

CMRR scales linearly with tail impedance RSS. A simple MOSFET tail has RSS = ro_M5 = 1/(λ·ISS). A cascode tail multiplies this by gm_c·ro_c, which can be 100× to 10,000×. The cascode improvement in dB equals 20·log10(gm_c·ro_c) — typically 40–60 dB additional rejection.

PSRR− body-effect limit
PSRR+ at DC = gm1·ro_M3 (simple mirror) or gm1·gm_c3·ro_c3·ro_M3 (cascode). It has a zero at fz = gm3/(2π·Cgd3) — beyond this frequency, VDD couples directly through Cgd3 to the output. PSRR− is degraded by the body effect: M1/M2 bodies are at VSS in standard n-well CMOS, creating a direct coupling path through γ·ΔVth that cannot be removed by improving M5. Use triple-well NMOS or a fully differential topology to improve PSRR−.
