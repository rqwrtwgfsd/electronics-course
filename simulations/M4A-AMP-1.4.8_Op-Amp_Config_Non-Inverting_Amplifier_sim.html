Gain and Bandwidth vs R2/R1 Ratio

Acl = 1 + R2/R1. As gain increases, bandwidth decreases. The product Acl × f_3dB = GBW. The minimum gain is 1 (buffer). The gain and bandwidth trade exactly linearly on this log-log plot.

Non-Inverting vs Inverting: Bandwidth at Same Gain

At the same gain magnitude, the non-inverting amplifier always has slightly wider bandwidth because its noise gain is 1 less than the inverting equivalent. This matters at low gains (2–10×).

Input Impedance vs Frequency

Zin(f) = Zcm / (1 + 1/T(f)). At DC, loop gain boosts Zin by T. At the GBW frequency, T = 1 and Zin ≈ Zcm/2. Above GBW, Zin = Zcm (just the bare op-amp input). Drag to see the transition.
