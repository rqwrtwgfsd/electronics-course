When the full op-amp has an output stage, CL at the Stage 2 node is just the internal capacitance (gate Cgs of M_p and M_n). The external CL creates a new pole fp3 at the output of the output stage. PM depends on both: PM = 90° − arctan(GBW/fp2_int) − arctan(GBW/fp3_ext). The output stage must have gm_out large enough to push fp3_ext well above GBW.

Each transistor's current depends on its VGS and the output voltage (which sets VDS). When Vout rises, VGS_n increases → M_n current rises; M_p current falls (VGS_p reduces). At Vout = VDD/2: both transistors carry the quiescent current IQ. The net output current (ID_p − ID_n) must supply the load.

THD vs Quiescent Current

THD (estimated)
Static power
Current IQ marker
THD in a class AB output stage decreases dramatically as IQ increases (quiescent current removes the crossover dead zone). Empirical model: THD ≈ A × (Ipeak/IQ)^B where A, B are topology-dependent (~A=1e-4, B=2 for well-designed class AB). Static power = VDD × IQ (for the output stage). There is a clear trade-off between low THD and low power. Target: IQ = 1–5% of Ipeak for audio-quality output; smaller IQ for power-critical battery devices.
