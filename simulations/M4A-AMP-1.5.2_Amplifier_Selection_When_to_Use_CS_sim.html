M4A-AMP-1.5.2 — Amplifier Selection: When to Use Common-Source

High-Z sourcing, intrinsic gain, Miller effect, and the CS vs CE vs op-amp trade space

Input Impedance vs Frequency: CS vs CE

CS gate impedance: Zin = 1/(jωCgs) — capacitive only, zero DC current. CE base impedance: β/gm at low frequency, dropping with frequency. At high-impedance sources, CS wins for f less than the CE pole.

CS Intrinsic Gain vs Drain Current

gm ∝ sqrt(ID); ro ∝ 1/ID. Intrinsic gain A0 = gm×ro ∝ 1/sqrt(ID). Lower bias gives higher intrinsic gain — but lower bandwidth and higher 1/f noise corner. Drag to see the tradeoff.

Effective Input Capacitance: CS vs CS+CG Cascode

Miller effect: Cin = Cgs + Cgd×(1+|Av|). Cascode (CG on top) eliminates Miller: Cin_casc ≈ Cgs + 2×Cgd. At high gain, the cascode dramatically reduces input capacitance and extends bandwidth.

CS: Cin = Cgs + Cgd×(1+|Av|)
Cascode: Cin ≈ Cgs + 2×Cgd
